On our first stop through time, we will be traveling to New York in the Ordovician period. During this time in pre-history, the Earth is much different than the one you know. Days are only 21 hours long and there is more carbon dioxide in the atmosphere than there is oxygen. To help prepare for this, Time Masters Inc. has developed specialized bio-suits equipped with a special air mixture to help you breathe. Once you step onto the land you will notice something very quickly; there is no life around. The reason for this is because the animals that live in the sea haven't evolved yet. It won't be until the Devonian period when this change happens. Until then, you will enjoy seeing species such as Orthocones, Sea Scorpions and Trilobites.
While this species of Orthocone known as Cameroceras is not the world's first sea monster, it certainly will stand out as the biggest of it's time. Don't worry, it won't harm us when you take the dive out to sea. As the top predator of Ordovician seas, Cameroceras often had to use its tentacles to catch sea scorpions and trilobites. Once the prey was caught, the Orthocone would use its beak to crush them to bits. The modern day equivalent to this creature would be the Giant Squid.
Did you know that scorpions used to be in the sea before they came on land and shrunk in size? This specific species called Megalograptus was one of many during the age before the dinosaurs. Belonging to a group of marine arthropods called eurypterids, they lacked a stinger but used their sharp spines to attack prey such as trilobites. You will see plenty of them on shore and in the water while on the tour.
