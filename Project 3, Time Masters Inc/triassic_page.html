On our third stop through time, we will be stopping in the ancient supercontinent of Pangaea. The Triassic was the time when the dinosaurs first appeared on the Earth. As they began to reign the Earth, other creatures co-existed with them. In the water, other creatures started out as the world's sea monsters before evolution created real prehistoric monsters of the Mesozoic. Since I'll be providing the information on the dinosaurs and other creatures of this time period, Time Masters Inc. has given you the opportunity to listen to what they sound like in the wild. Some of the images were taken in the wild by our professional photographer.
Though primitive, Cymbospondylus is one of the largest ichthyosaurus to ever exist. See the tail of this creature? Cymbospondylus would often use it to achieve great speed in order to catch prey both in shallow and deep water. Unlike other marine life, Cymbospondylus gave birth to live young. This is a trait that dolphins and whales have today. If this creature comes to close to you while on the tour, make sure to use a prod to keep it at bay.
A common marine reptile, Nothosaurus was quite the sight back in its day. This creature is semi-aquatic because it would sometimes come on to land, but it mostly spent time in the water where it hunted for fish. Once prey is caught in a Nothosaur's grip, there is no escape. There is something risky you could try on the tour, if you grab one by the jaws, then you'll have a ride with it through the water. Make sure it goes up for air though.
Take a look at this. With a long neck and tail, Tanystropheus had the greatest length of any marine reptile during this time period. Though perfect for ambush predators, this marine reptile has an advantage. If a predator catches the tail, Tanystropheus will drop it like modern lizards do. Don't worry, the tail will grow back. We highly advise you to not disturb these creatures because we don't want any tails dropped.
