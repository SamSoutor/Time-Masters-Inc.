On our fourth stop through time, we will truly see more dinosaurs in the flesh during the Jurassic period. This time period is considered to be the era when the dinosaurs became the dominant creatures of the Earth. Species such as Allosaurus, Diplodocus and Stegosaurus were some of the biggest to ever walk during the Jurassic. Out at sea, prehistoric sea monsters reigned supreme. Since I'll be providing the information on the dinosaurs, Time Masters Inc. has given you the opportunity to listen to what they sound like in the wild. The Stegosaurus image was taken in the wild by our professional photographer.
The apex predator of the Jurassic seas, Liopleurodon was feared by all sea creatures including sharks. With large jaws and a sense of smell that makes other predators seem inferior, this sea monster showed no mercy. To make sure that no adults attack us on the tour, we've installed an advanced system which releases a mixture of chemicals to drive them away.
Back home we have saltwater crocodiles, here we have Metriorhynchus. A marine crocodile that would often scavenge large carcasses or hunt small marine life, this predator lived under the shadow of Liopleurodon. It also has a fish-like tail which it moved side-to-side. This is similar to how modern crocodiles move. To keep them from attacking you out at sea, make sure to use a prod.
While there may be marine reptiles, there are also fish in the Jurassic seas. The biggest of them would have to be the Leedsichthys. These fish would travel in groups to feast on plankton. If one of them fell behind, they would become targets to the predators that lurked in the deep. Open water dives with these creatures should be safe, just make sure that you don't fall behind the group, otherwise you'll be seen by Liopleurodon as a snack.
